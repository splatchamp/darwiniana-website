You may hear evolution and natural selection used interchangeably. Evolution is merely a multi-generational change in allele frequencies in a given population of organisms. Natural selection, discovered concurrently by Charles Darwin and Alfred Russel Wallace, is an adaptive form of evolution - one which contributes positively to an organism's reproductive output. As Steven Pinker puts it, "Natural selection is not the only process that changes organisms over time. But is the only process that seemingly designs organisms over time." What, then, are the other forms of evolution - the non-adaptive ones? Non-adaptive forms of evolution include genetic drift, gene flow, and mutation."

Camouflage is a product of natural selection favored by teachers in a classroom. “Behavioral genetics confirms that aggressive tendencies can be inherited, and that gives natural selection material to work with in shifting the average violent tendencies of a population.” - Steven Pinker
